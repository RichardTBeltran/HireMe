About Me

I am a Performance Science Intern for the Tampa Bay Rays. Previously I was a graduate assistant at the University of Memphis, School of Health Sciences, where I obtained my Masters Degree in Exercise, Sports and Movement Science with an Emphasis in Biomechanics. I worked in the Musculoskeletal Lab at the University of Memphis under Dr. Max Paquette. My research focuses on running biomechanics, more specifically the impact that footwear plays in running biomechanics. My thesis topic was on the effect of high longitudinal bending stiffness shoes on older runners biomechanics and running economy. I received a Bachelor of Science in Exercise Science from the University of Puget Sound. There, I was advised by Dr. Michael Pohl and investigated the effects of different golf stances on the knee adduction moment. I am passionate about all sports and want to research different way to improve performance and reduce the risk of injury. Finally, I was a collegiate golfer at the Division III level
