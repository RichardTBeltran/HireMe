RESEARCH
Footwear effects of in-shoe force and ground reaction force during running.
Richard T. Beltran, Max R. Paquette (2020)
• Examines the difference between in-shoe force and ground reaction force during running
• In-Shoe force was measured using wearable force insoles (Novel)
• Ground reaction force was measure using an instrumented force treadmill (Bertec)
• Data was analyzed using Visual3D and SPSS

Effects of minimal, traditional and maximal shoes on lower limb mechanics in rear and non-rearfoot strike runners.
Richard T. Beltran, Jake A. Melaro, Isabel Moore, Max R. Paquette (2019)
• Examines the effect of different types of shoes on running related injury biomechanics
• Kinematics measured using Vicon 3D Motion Capture System
• Data was analyzed using Visual3D and SPSS

Are all training loads created equal?
Megan R. Ryan, Tayler M. Vickery, Adriana Miltko, Richard T. Beltran, Chris Napier, Max R. Paquette. (2019)
• Examine the relationship between bone stimulus, rate of perceived exertion, cumulative force and step count
• Bone stimulus (Tibial Acceleration) and step count was measured using IMeasureU accelerometers
• Cumulative force was measured using Novel wearable force insoles
